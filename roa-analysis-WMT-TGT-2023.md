# ROA Analysis: Walmart vs Target (2023)

Using fiscal year `2023` data from `calvinw/BusMgmtBenchmarks/main`.

| Metric | Walmart | Target |
|--------|---------:|-------:|
| Net Revenue | 648,125,000 | 107,412,000 |
| Cost of Goods | 490,142,000 | 77,736,000 |
| Gross Margin | 157,983,000 | 29,676,000 |
| SGA | 130,971,000 | 21,554,000 |
| SGA % of Revenue | 20.21% | 20.07% |
| Operating Profit | 27,012,000 | 5,707,000 |

| Total Assets | 252,399,000 | 55,356,000 |

## ROA Breakdown

| Metric | Walmart | Target |
|--------|---------:|-------:|
| Net Profit Margin % | 2.39% | 3.85% |
| x Asset Turnover | 2.57 | 1.94 |
| = ROA % | 6.15% | 7.48% |

## Interpretation

Target has the higher ROA in 2023, which means it generated more profit per dollar of assets overall. Target is also more profitable per dollar of sales, with a higher net profit margin than Walmart. Walmart uses its assets more efficiently, though, with much higher asset turnover. SG&A helps explain how much gross margin gets consumed by operating costs, and here both companies were very similar on that measure, with SG&A running at about 20% of revenue for each. The DuPont breakdown shows the contrast clearly: Walmart's model relies on very high sales volume relative to assets with thin margins, while Target earns stronger margins but turns its assets over more slowly.
